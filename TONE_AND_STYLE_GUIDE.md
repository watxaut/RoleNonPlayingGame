# Role Non-Playing Game - Tone & Style Guide

**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** 2025-11-13
**Status:** Living Document

---

## Core Tone Philosophy

**Role Non-Playing Game** strikes a balance between serious fantasy adventure and self-aware humor.
The
world of Aethermoor is genuinely dangerous and mysterious, but the game doesn't take itself so
seriously that it can't have fun along the way.

### The Balance

- **70% Serious**: The world, lore, and core narrative are treated with respect. Real stakes, real
  danger, real mystery.
- **30% Humor**: Characters, items, and situations include moments of levity, absurdity, and playful
  references.

**Key Principle**: The humor should feel natural and earned, not forced. It emerges from character
personalities, unexpected situations, and the inherent absurdity of autonomous adventuring.

## Writing Guidelines

### 1. World & Lore (Serious)

The foundational elements remain grounded:

**DO:**

- Treat the Sundering, the eternal storms, and ancient civilizations with gravitas
- Write location descriptions with atmospheric detail
- Give legendary monsters and bosses genuine threat and presence
- Maintain internal consistency in the world's rules

**DON'T:**

- Undercut major plot points with jokes
- Break the fourth wall in lore texts (save that for NPCs and items)
- Make every situation comedic

**Example:**

```
The Bone Empress (Serious)
"The preserved corpse of an ancient desert queen, animated by necromantic magic.
She rules the Bone Canyons from her tomb throne, commanding skeletal warriors
and mummified guardians. Her crown is said to grant power over death itself."
```

---

### 2. NPCs & Dialogue (Serious with Humor)

Characters should feel alive, which means they have personalities, quirks, and opinions.

**Types of NPCs:**

**The Straight Players** (Mostly Serious)

- Quest givers with genuine problems
- Faction leaders
- Mysterious lore keepers
- Example: "The storms grow fiercer each year. I fear something is changing..."

**The Comic Relief** (Mostly Humor)

- Bumbling merchants
- Overconfident adventurers
- Sarcastic townsfolk
- Example: "Another hero, eh? Let me guess—you're going to save us all? The last three are still
  stuck in the Webbed Hollow."

**The Self-Aware** (Balanced)

- NPCs who acknowledge the strangeness of their world
- Veterans who've seen it all
- Example: "A warrior who puts all their points into Luck? Bold strategy. Stupid, but bold."

**Dialogue Principles:**

- Characters can be funny without being jokes
- Humor comes from personality, not randomness
- NPCs can reference game mechanics in-character ("You look like you could use some healing potions.
  And maybe a few more points in Vitality.")

**Example Conversations:**

```
[Innkeeper in Havenmoor]
"Welcome to the Rusty Tankard! Safest inn on the island. Of course, it's also IN the safest
town on the island, so that's not saying much. Room for the night?"

[Veteran Ranger at Thornguard Outpost]
"You planning to explore the deep Thornwood? *gestures to wall of memorial plaques*
We keep a list. For... administrative purposes."

[Goblin Merchant (rare encounter)]
"Yes, yes, I know what you're thinking. 'Goblins are enemies!' But I'm a BUSINESSMAN goblin.
Very different. Now, you want to buy this shiny stick or not?"
```

### 3. Items & Equipment (Where Humor Shines)

Item descriptions are prime territory for personality and humor, especially for common/useless
items.

**Item Tiers & Tone:**

**Legendary Items** (Serious)

- Powerful artifacts deserve epic descriptions
- Lore-heavy, mysterious
- Example: *"Frostbite, Blade of the Last Dwarf King - Forged in the heart of Irondelve before
  the vanishing. The blade never dulls, never warms, and whispers of vengeance."*

**Epic/Rare Items** (Serious with flavor)

- Strong equipment with personality
- Can hint at backstory
- Example: *"Champion's Greatsword - This blade has seen better days, but so have you. +15 STR,
  +10 VIT. 'Heavy is the sword that doesn't miss arm day.' - Inscription on hilt"*

**Uncommon Items** (Balanced)

- Practical gear with character
- Example: *"Leather Boots of Minor Swiftness - They're boots. They're brown. They make you
  slightly faster. What more do you want?"*

**Common/Junk Items** (Humor encouraged)

- Perfect for jokes and references
- Where most Easter eggs live
- Example: *"Definitely Not a Buster Sword - A comically oversized sword that's definitely
  original and not from any spiky-haired protagonist's arsenal. Too heavy to actually use.
  Sell value: 1 gil... er, gold."*

**More Item Examples:**

```
"Pointy Stick" (Common)
"It's a stick. It's pointy. Your character picked it up for some reason. Maybe they see
potential you don't. Damage: 1-2"

"Potion of Questionable Healing" (Uncommon)
"Restores 50 HP. The label says 'Best Before: Third Age.' You're currently in the Seventh Age.
Your character doesn't seem to care."

"Extremely Rusty Sword" (Common)
"This sword has seen combat. A lot of combat. Mostly against rust. Damage: 3-5.
Tetanus chance: High"

"Amulet of +1 Charisma" (Uncommon)
"Makes you 1% more likeable. Your character wears it proudly anyway.
We don't have the heart to tell them."

"The One Ring" (Legendary... or is it?)
"A simple gold band. Makes you invisible! ...Just kidding, it's a regular ring.
+1 LUK because you're lucky we have a sense of humor. Definitely not cursed. Probably."

"Orb of Slope Detection" (Rare)
"Tells you which way is downhill. Incredibly accurately. Useful for... rolling?
Scholars debate its true purpose."

"Stick of Infinite Fetch" (Uncommon)
"Throw it, and wolves become friendly temporarily. They just want to play.
'Who's a good dire wolf? You are!' Achievement unlocked when equipped."
```

### 4. Quest Text (Serious premise, room for humor)

Quests should have genuine objectives but can be delivered with personality.

**Serious Quests:**

```
[Quest: The Missing Expedition]
"A dwarven expedition entered Irondelve Hold three weeks ago. They haven't returned.
The Irondelve Reclaimers need someone to investigate their fate—and recover their research
if possible."

Objective: Search Irondelve Hold for the missing expedition
Reward: 500 XP, 200 Gold, Dwarven Map Fragment
```

**Humorous Quests:**

```
[Quest: Rats. Why Did It Have to Be Rats?]
"The innkeeper is desperate. The basement is FULL of rats. Giant rats.
He swears he only saw one last week. 'They're multiplying faster than my debts!'
he cried. Please help."

Objective: Clear the inn basement of rats (0/20)
Reward: 100 XP, 50 Gold, Innkeeper's Gratitude (and probably some cheese)

[Quest: The Great Slime Debate]
"A scholar in Havenmoor is conducting research on slime intelligence. He needs fresh samples.
He assures you this is 'legitimate science' and not 'an excuse to make you fight slimes.'
You have doubts."

Objective: Collect Slime Cores (0/10)
Reward: 150 XP, 75 Gold, "Slime Scientist" Title
```

**Easter Egg Quests:**

```
[Quest: A Rumor of Chocobos] (Hidden)
"An old traveler swears he saw large, yellow, flightless birds in the Heartlands.
Everyone thinks he's crazy. Maybe investigate anyway?"

Objective: Search for the mythical "Chocobo" sightings
Reward: ??? (spoiler: you find "Definitely Not Chocobos" - yellow chickens)

[Quest: The Dungeon Master's Challenge] (Hidden)
"A mysterious robed figure offers you a challenge: Roll for initiative.
Wait, wrong game. He wants you to clear a dungeon using ONLY dice-luck.
No skills, just raw LUK stat checks."

Objective: Complete the Random Dungeon with 20+ LUK
Reward: Twenty-Sided Die (Legendary Accessory), "Critical Success" Title
```

### 5. Activity Log Entries (Mostly Serious, Occasional Humor)

The activity log should be informative but can have personality based on what happened.

**Standard Entries:**

```
"Defeated Level 5 Goblin Warrior (+50 XP, +12 Gold)"
"Discovered: The Webbed Hollow"
"Accepted Quest: Clear the Rats"
"Reached Level 10! Allocated points: +2 STR, +1 VIT"
```

**Unusual Situations:**

```
"Attempted to fight Level 35 Sandreaver. Ran away VERY fast. Survived. Barely."
"Found 'Definitely Not a Buster Sword'. Too heavy to equip. Sold it immediately. Wise choice."
"Met player character 'xXDarkLord420Xx'. Chose not to engage. Understandable."
"Equipped 'Amulet of +1 Charisma'. Felt slightly more confident. Results pending."
"Defeated by a rat. ...A REALLY big rat. Level 3 Giant Rat. Still embarrassing."
```

**Critical Moments (Always Serious):**

```
"RARE DROP: Frostbite, Blade of the Last Dwarf King acquired!"
"ACHIEVEMENT UNLOCKED: Dragon Slayer - Defeated Hoarfang, the Glacier Drake"
"Discovered LORE FRAGMENT: The Truth of the Sundering (5/10)"
"DEATH: Defeated by Thornmaw, the Corrupted Treant. Respawned in Havenmoor. Lost 147 Gold."
```

## Easter Eggs & References

### Guidelines for References

**DO:**

- Make references subtle enough that missing them doesn't hurt the experience
- Transform references to fit Aethermoor's world
- Use references as flavor, not plot points
- Spread them across items, NPC names, and hidden quests

**DON'T:**

- Make direct, obvious references that break immersion
- Copy names exactly (transform them slightly)
- Rely on references for core content

### Reference Categories

**Final Fantasy Homages:**

- Items: "Definitely Not a Buster Sword", "Spiky Hair Gel" (cosmetic), "Moogle Pendant"
  (whispers "Kupo")
- NPCs: A merchant named "Sid" who's really good at machines
- Locations: "Crystal Caves" with glowing crystals, hidden "Chocobo" chickens
- Enemies: "Behemoth" (but it's called "Behemooth" and is slightly cow-like)

**D&D References:**

- The "Dungeon Master's Challenge" quest
- "Twenty-Sided Die" legendary accessory (all stats +1d20)
- NPC party with classic composition: Fighter, Cleric, Wizard, Rogue
- "Dice Set of Many Things" (consumable, random effect)
- "Bag of Holding" (actually just a normal bag, false advertising)

**Classic RPG Tropes:**

- "Potion Seller's Strongest Potion" (reference to the meme, but actually just a strong potion)
- "This Sword Is Not Legendary" (It's Epic tier, feels cheated)
- NPCs who acknowledge the "Hero Loop" of arriving, getting quests, leveling up
- A "Chicken Chaser" title for characters who attack peaceful chickens

**Other Games:**

- "Praise the Sun Amulet" (religion on Aethermoor worships the sun)
- "Cake Is a Lie" (quest reward, gives no cake, gives pie instead)
- "Arrow to the Knee Guard" (protects knees, prevents early retirement)

## Achievement Names & Descriptions

Achievements are great for humor since they're meta by nature.

**Serious Achievements:**

```
"Dragon Slayer" - Defeat your first dragon
"Master of the Elements" - Defeat all four elemental bosses
"Lorekeeper" - Discover all lore fragments
"Legend" - Reach Level 50
```

**Humorous Achievements:**

```
"It's Dangerous to Go Alone" - Complete first quest
"Curiosity Killed the Adventurer" - Die to a monster 10+ levels above you
"Not the Bees!" - Get defeated by bee-type enemies 5 times
"Hoarder" - Inventory completely full of junk items
"Social Butterfly" - Interact with 50 different player characters
"Bold Strategy, Cotton" - Attempt to fight a World Boss before Level 20
"Running in the 90s" - Flee from 100 combats
"Stick Collector" - Acquire 10+ wooden stick variants
"LEEROY JENKINS!" - Your character rushes into a boss room without preparation
"Natural 20" - Achieve a critical hit with 20+ LUK stat
"Task Failed Successfully" - Complete a quest in the most inefficient way possible
```

## Monster Names & Descriptions

Monsters follow the same tier system:

**World Bosses & Legendary Enemies** (Serious)

- Thornmaw, the Corrupted Treant
- Sandreaver, the Eternal Wyrm
- Tempest, the Storm Leviathan

**Standard Enemies** (Can be playful)

- "Suspiciously Large Rat" (it's just a big rat)
- "Definitely a Mimic" (a chest that's obviously a monster, your character tries anyway)
- "Confused Skeleton" (keeps dropping its own bones)
- "Slime (Blue)" vs "Slime (Red)" (Red ones are spicy)
- "Murder Hobo" (bandit enemy, on-the-nose RPG joke)

**Rare/Easter Egg Enemies** (Humor encouraged)

- "The Rabbit of Caerbannog" (extremely dangerous rabbit, only appears rarely)
- "Cactaur" (cactus creature that runs away quickly)
- "Tonberry" (small creature with huge damage, slow movement)

## NPC Naming Conventions

**Serious Names** (Faction leaders, important lore figures):

- Titles: The Frost Tyrant, The Bone Empress, The First Wanderer
- Fantasy names: Aldric, Theron, Lysandra, Kaelen

**Playful Names** (Merchants, comic relief):

- Puns: "Justin Time" (traveling merchant), "Paige Turner" (librarian)
- References: "Sid" (mechanic), "Biggs & Wedge" (guard duo)
- Descriptive: "Old Man Henderson" (crazy hermit with wild stories)

**Player Character Names** (AI encounters):

- The game should acknowledge that players name their characters anything
- NPCs can comment on unusual names
- Example: NPC meets "xXSephirothXx" - "Unusual name. Your parents must have been... creative."

## Writing Checklist

When writing any content, ask:

1. **Does this fit the world?** (Even jokes should feel grounded)
2. **Is the tone appropriate for the content tier?** (Legendary items = serious, common items =
   flexible)
3. **Would this make someone smile without breaking immersion?** (Good humor enhances, doesn't
   destroy)
4. **Is the reference subtle enough?** (Transform it, don't copy it)
5. **Does the serious content have weight?** (Don't undercut important moments)

## Examples of Tone Balance

### Good Balance ✓

**Item: "Hero's Blade"**

```
"A legendary sword passed down through generations. Its edge never dulls, its balance
is perfect. The inscription reads: 'To whoever is brave enough—or dumb enough—to wield me.'
Apparently, even legendary swords have doubts."

+25 STR, +15 VIT, Special: Heroic Strike ability
```

*Why it works: Serious item with genuine power, but the inscription adds personality without
undermining the blade's importance.*

### Poor Balance ✗

**Item: "Legendary Sword of Memes"**

```
"LOL this sword is so random! It's made of pure memes. Damage: 69. Nice.
Such wow. Very blade. #Blessed"
```

*Why it fails: Breaks immersion completely, relies entirely on memes, doesn't fit the world at all.*

---

### Good Balance ✓

**Quest: "The Hermit's Request"**

```
"An old hermit living in the Thornwood needs rare mushrooms for his 'research.'
He's either a genius alchemist or completely insane. Possibly both.
The mushrooms are guarded by territorial treants."

Objective: Collect Rare Mushrooms (0/5)
Reward: 300 XP, Mystery Potion, "Mushroom Forager" Title
```

*Why it works: Legitimate quest with clear objective, but the hermit's ambiguous nature adds
character.*

### Poor Balance ✗

**Quest: "YOLO Swag Quest"**

```
"Some random dude wants you to do random stuff because YOLO. Go fight things.
Or don't. Whatever. #NoFilter"

Objective: ???
Reward: Memes
```

*Why it fails: No world-building, no stakes, pure randomness without purpose.*

## Conclusion

**Role Non-Playing Game** should make players smile while they watch their character's journey, but
never at the expense of the world's credibility. The game is self-aware enough to have fun, but
serious enough to create genuine investment in the world of Aethermoor.

**The Golden Rule**:
When in doubt, ask: "Would this make me laugh AND respect the game world?"
If yes → proceed. If no → revise.
